# Project Improvement Ideas

## Easy Wins (low effort, high value)

1. Publication date shown on each source in the "Official sources" expander
2. Quick question buttons — pre-typed common questions users can tap (no typing needed)
3. Emergency contacts with actual phone numbers in the sidebar (OSAR, SEM hotline, cantonal services)
4. "Last updated" timestamp in sidebar showing when the database was last fetched

## Content & UX Improvements

5. Latest news widget in sidebar — show 3 most recent headlines from the database
6. Permit quick-reference card — collapsible table comparing N, F, B, C, S side by side (work rights, duration, family reunification, etc.)
7. Language selector — let users explicitly choose their language instead of relying on auto-detection
8. Step-by-step arrival guide — "What do I do first when I arrive in Switzerland?" as a structured flow, not just Q&A

## Advanced Features

9.  Voice input — speak your question instead of typing, critical for users who cannot read/write or are not comfortable typing in a foreign language
10. Deadline calculator — enter a key date (hearing date, appeal deadline, permit expiry) and get a timeline of what to do and when
11. SEM letter reader — upload a document or photo of a letter from SEM, get a plain-language explanation of what it means and what action is required

## Technical Improvements

12. Use the feedback data (thumbs up/down already collected) to identify which topics give bad answers and improve sources
13. Semantic / vector search — currently keyword-based; semantic search would find relevant sources even when the user uses different words ("can I work?" matching "employment rights")
14. Confidence indicator — when the LLM is answering from limited sources, flag it clearly instead of sounding certain
15. Analytics view — how many questions asked, what topics, which cantons, feedback scores over time
